# Editorial Guide

## Purpose

pbrentyoung.com is not a portfolio.

It is a thinking platform.

The work exists to support the ideas.

The ideas become the primary product.

Visitors should leave with the impression that Brent doesn't simply make beautiful things. He helps churches and mission-driven organizations think clearly about communication.

---

# Mission

Helping churches and mission-driven organizations communicate with clarity through story, design, leadership, and systems.

---

# Positioning

Creative Director

Brand Strategist

Storyteller

Systems Thinker

Teacher

Problem Solver

The common thread is helping organizations tell the right story, clearly and intentionally.

---

# Core Beliefs

Communication is ministry.

Design is hospitality.

Brand builds trust.

Marketing removes unnecessary obstacles.

Great creativity requires great systems.

Good communication begins with listening.

Story is more important than style.

Design solves creative problems.

Creative work begins by defining the problem.

Systems create margin for creativity.

Campaigns contain Projects.

Projects contain Deliverables.

Deliverables contain Tasks.

---

# Editorial Voice

Warm.

Thoughtful.

Reflective.

Curious.

Practical.

Observant.

Teacher more than expert.

Coffee conversation more than keynote speech.

Confident without arrogance.

---

# Writing Style

Use stories.

Use real examples.

Use short paragraphs.

Ask questions.

Avoid jargon.

Teach principles instead of tactics.

Leave room for curiosity.

Every article should challenge assumptions before offering solutions.

---

# What We Avoid

Corporate language.

Marketing clichés.

Clickbait.

Buzzwords.

Artificial urgency.

"Top 10"

"Hacks"

"Secrets"

Thought leader language.

---

# Design Philosophy

Design is not decoration.

Design is problem solving.

The visual result is evidence that the communication problem was understood.

---

# Before / After

Every article should illustrate transformation.

Transformation may be visual.

Transformation may be conceptual.

Transformation may be organizational.

The reader should clearly understand:

Before

↓

After

---

# Human vs AI

AI generates outputs.

Design defines problems.

The future belongs to people who ask better questions before generating better answers.

---

# Every Article Should Leave The Reader Thinking

"I've never looked at it that way before."

Not

"Those were good tips."
