Research Plan: John 4 and the Journey to Transformation

Biblical Text

Primary passage: John 4:1–42

Study goals:  
• Follow the structure and movement of the encounter  
• Distinguish textual observation from later application  
• Identify repeated words, questions, shifts in address, and changes in agency  
• Compare the woman’s testimony with the town’s eventual confession

Historical Context

Research:  
• Jewish and Samaritan history and tensions  
• The significance of traveling through Samaria  
• Jacob’s well and the geography of Sychar  
• Social customs surrounding wells, hospitality, gender, and public conversation  
• The woman’s likely social setting without overstating what the text does not say  
• Samaritan worship and the Mount Gerizim/Jerusalem dispute

Theological Themes

• Living water  
• Worship in spirit and truth  
• Revelation and recognition of Jesus  
• Testimony  
• Harvest  
• Mission across boundaries  
• Personal dignity, conviction, and transformation

Communication and Marketing Research

• Customer journey models  
• Personas and empathy mapping  
• Touchpoint design  
• Pain points and felt needs  
• Progressive commitment  
• Calls to action  
• Trust formation  
• Word of mouth and advocacy  
• Community and network effects  
• Behavioral outcomes versus vanity metrics

Ministry Application Research

Collect examples from:  
• Church websites and first-time guest journeys  
• Social media and digital outreach  
• Guest services and physical signage  
• Follow-up systems  
• Small groups and discipleship pathways  
• Testimony capture and member invitation  
• Communications that address only awareness while neglecting later stages

Source Standards

• Prioritize peer-reviewed biblical scholarship and respected commentaries  
• Preserve multiple responsible interpretations where scholarship differs  
• Avoid claims about the woman’s morality, social exclusion, or time at the well that the text cannot firmly support  
• Cite primary marketing research where possible  
• Separate observation, interpretation, and application in working notes

Research Questions by Article

1\. What does “had to pass through Samaria” mean narratively and theologically?  
2\. What does Jesus know about the woman, and when is it revealed?  
3\. How does the water conversation create shared language?  
4\. What makes the opening request socially unexpected?  
5\. Where does the conversation deepen, and who initiates each shift?  
6\. How does Jesus combine truth and dignity?  
7\. How does the promise of living water reshape the conversation?  
8\. What is the narrative significance of leaving the jar?  
9\. Why is “Come and see” persuasive?  
10\. How does the town move from her testimony to its own belief?

Deliverables

• Annotated passage outline  
• Source bibliography  
• Research notes for each article  
• Theological guardrails  
• Case-study library  
• Quote and illustration ban

AI-Assisted Persona Research

Research thesis: Personas should be discovered from real stories and real behavior, not invented from staff assumptions.

Proposed pilot study:  
• Recruit the last 20 people who joined the church, attended a membership class, joined a group, began volunteering, returned after a long absence, or chose not to return.  
• Conduct short, structured audio interviews using the same open-ended question set.  
• Obtain explicit consent for recording and research use.  
• Transcribe and remove identifying details.  
• Store the de-identified transcripts in NotebookLM or another secure, approved AI research environment.

Questions for participants:  
• What was happening in your life when you first looked for a church?  
• Where did you search or ask for information?  
• What almost kept you from coming?  
• What helped you feel safe or comfortable?  
• What was confusing or unexpectedly difficult?  
• What helped you take the next step?  
• What language connected with you?  
• What language felt unfamiliar, institutional, or insider-focused?  
• Which moments built trust?  
• Which moments created friction?

AI analysis prompts should explore:  
• Recurring motivations and felt needs  
• Common fears, objections, and barriers  
• Language participants naturally use  
• First and most influential touchpoints  
• Trust-building moments  
• Friction and abandonment points  
• Decision triggers and progressive commitments  
• Differences between invited guests, search-driven guests, returning Christians, and people in crisis  
• Contradictions between staff assumptions and participant experience  
• Outlier stories that challenge the dominant pattern

Expected outputs:  
• Evidence-based persona profiles  
• Journey-stage summaries  
• Touchpoint inventory  
• Pain-point and friction map  
• Trust and decision-trigger map  
• Recommendations for website, social media, guest experience, follow-up, groups, and serving pathways  
• Source-linked findings that can be checked against the original interviews

Ethical and pastoral safeguards:  
• Use AI to understand people, never to exploit them.  
• Do not upload counseling notes, prayer requests, medical information, abuse disclosures, or other sensitive pastoral data.  
• Do not infer trauma, mental-health diagnoses, finances, family status, or spiritual condition.  
• De-identify transcripts before analysis.  
• Limit access to the research workspace.  
• Preserve original recordings and transcripts according to an approved retention policy.  
• Report patterns without erasing minority or outlier experiences.  
• Validate AI summaries against source interviews before using them.  
• Treat personas as provisional research tools, never as stereotypes or fixed identities.

Research method:  
Listen → Transcribe → De-identify → Organize → Identify Patterns → Build Personas → Map Touchpoints → Validate → Improve the Journey

Evaluation questions:  
• Did the research reveal needs or obstacles staff had not recognized?  
• Can each persona claim be traced to actual interviews?  
• Do proposed changes improve dignity, clarity, accessibility, and trust?  
• Are we using the findings to serve people more faithfully rather than merely increase response rates?
