Evangelistic Marketing: Working Thesis

People do not arrive at transformation by accident.

Jesus intentionally guided people through encounters that built trust, revealed truth, and invited response. Modern marketers call this a customer journey. Scripture shows something deeper: a journey toward transformation.

The Woman at the Well in John 4 provides a complete picture of that journey. Jesus goes to the marketplace rather than waiting for someone to come to Him. He meets a specific person rather than addressing an abstract demographic. He begins with her immediate reality, creates a low-friction first touchpoint, earns the right to deepen the conversation, speaks truth without manipulation, offers a compelling vision of new life, and allows transformation to become advocacy.

This project does not argue that Jesus was practicing commercial marketing. It argues that the best principles of communication, empathy, persuasion, invitation, and advocacy are echoes of something Jesus understood perfectly: people move toward change through relationship, meaning, trust, and truth.

Central Argument

The church often builds a well and waits for people to find it. Jesus went through Samaria.

Faithful communication therefore requires us to:

• Go where people already are  
• Understand the people we hope to serve  
• Recognize their pain points and felt needs  
• Design meaningful touchpoints across the whole journey  
• Earn the ask rather than demanding it  
• Communicate truth with clarity and grace  
• Make the next faithful step visible  
• Equip transformed people to say, “Come and see”

The goal is not conversion as a metric. The goal is transformation as the fruit of faithful presence, clear communication, and the work of God.

Book Connection

This framework should become a central section of Evangelistic Marketing: Building a Church Brand That Resonates. It can also function as the narrative spine connecting personas, touchpoints, brand, customer journeys, hospitality, trust, calls to action, advocacy, and community.
