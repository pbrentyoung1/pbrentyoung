Journey Framework: John 4

Purpose

This document maps the movement of John 4 to the language of communication and ministry. The framework is descriptive, not reductive. It does not turn conversion into a funnel. It helps communicators recognize that people encounter truth through a sequence of relationships, experiences, questions, and invitations.

The Journey

1\. Jesus enters Samaria  
Communication: Marketplace presence  
Ministry: Mission and incarnation  
Question: Are we going where people already are?

2\. Jesus meets a specific woman  
Communication: Persona and audience understanding  
Ministry: Personhood and dignity  
Question: Do we see a person or only a demographic?

3\. Jesus asks for a drink  
Communication: First touchpoint  
Ministry: Hospitality and mutuality  
Question: Is the first interaction human, clear, and low-friction?

4\. The conversation turns to thirst  
Communication: Pain point and felt need  
Ministry: Empathy and discernment  
Question: Are we listening for the need beneath the request?

5\. Jesus offers living water  
Communication: Value proposition and promise  
Ministry: Gospel and hope  
Question: Can people imagine the future we are inviting them toward?

6\. Jesus reveals what He knows  
Communication: Trust, truth, and earned depth  
Ministry: Conviction and repentance  
Question: Have we earned the right to make a deeper ask?

7\. The conversation turns to worship  
Communication: Reframing and vision  
Ministry: Identity and revelation  
Question: Are we answering only the presenting question or the deeper one?

8\. She leaves her water jar  
Communication: Behavioral outcome  
Ministry: Transformation and new identity  
Question: What visible change follows the encounter?

9\. She tells the town, “Come and see”  
Communication: Advocacy and word of mouth  
Ministry: Testimony and evangelism  
Question: Can transformed people easily invite others?

10\. The town comes and believes  
Communication: Network effect and community journey  
Ministry: Harvest and church  
Question: Are we communicating well to every stage, not just the first one?

Canonical Sequence

Marketplace → Person → Touchpoint → Pain → Promise → Trust → Truth → Transformation → Advocacy → Community

Guardrails

• The Holy Spirit transforms; communication does not manufacture conversion.  
• People are never leads to be captured. They are neighbors to be loved.  
• A persona is an empathy tool, not a stereotype.  
• Pain points must never be exploited.  
• Earning the ask means honoring agency and trust.  
• Advocacy should emerge from authentic transformation, not pressure.
