Glossary: Evangelistic Marketing and John 4

Advocacy  
A person voluntarily sharing an experience, invitation, or testimony because it has become meaningful to them. In John 4, the woman’s “Come and see” is authentic advocacy.

Brand  
The accumulated meaning people attach to an organization through what it says, what it does, and how consistently those two things align.

Call to Action  
A clear invitation to take the next appropriate step. A faithful call to action respects agency and matches the trust already established.

Community  
The relational environment in which transformation is recognized, strengthened, and multiplied. The journey in John 4 moves from one person to a town.

Customer Journey  
A marketing term for the sequence of interactions through which a person moves from awareness to engagement, decision, and advocacy. In this project, it is reframed as a journey toward transformation.

Earning the Ask  
Building enough clarity, relevance, and trust before requesting a deeper commitment. It is not manipulation. It is respect for the person and the relationship.

Evangelistic Marketing  
The faithful stewardship of communication to understand people, meet them where they are, clearly express truth and hope, and invite them toward transformation without coercion.

Felt Need  
A need a person can currently recognize or articulate. Felt needs are often the doorway into deeper questions, but they should not be confused with the whole person.

Hospitality  
The practice of reducing unnecessary barriers and helping people know where they are, what is happening, and what they can do next.

Marketplace  
The physical or digital places where people already live, gather, work, search, and form meaning. Going to the marketplace means refusing to wait passively for people to enter church-controlled spaces.

Pain Point  
A frustration, fear, obstacle, or unmet need experienced by a person. Pain points should be understood with empathy, never exploited.

Persona  
A research-based representation used to help communicators understand a particular group’s context, needs, questions, motivations, and barriers. A persona serves personhood; it does not replace it.

Promise  
A clear articulation of the meaningful future, value, or hope being offered. In Christian communication, the promise must remain truthful and cannot guarantee outcomes God has not promised.

Stewardship  
The faithful investment of resources, attention, trust, influence, and opportunity for the good of others and the mission entrusted to us.

Touchpoint  
Any interaction through which a person experiences an organization or message, including websites, signage, conversations, social posts, events, emails, environments, and follow-up.

Transformation  
A meaningful change in understanding, identity, relationship, or behavior. It is deeper than a click, attendance count, or transaction.

Trust  
Confidence built through consistency, dignity, honesty, relevance, and demonstrated care. Trust creates room for deeper conversation but never entitles an organization to control the response.

Truth After Trust  
A shorthand for the pattern of speaking honestly within a relationship marked by dignity and attention. It does not imply delaying truth deceptively or avoiding it indefinitely.

Value Proposition  
A concise expression of why an invitation matters to the person receiving it. For ministry, this language must be handled carefully so the gospel is never reduced to a consumer benefit.
