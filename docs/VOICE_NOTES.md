# Voice Notes

Running list of Brent's voice and style rules, captured as we edit. These apply to
**every post on the site**, not just the one being worked on. When a new rule is
agreed, add it here so it stays consistent across sessions.

This file complements EDITORIAL_GUIDE.md (the "why") with the concrete "how."

---

## Point of view

- Talk about **"your brand," not "a brand."** Address the reader directly: you,
  your team, your desk, your church. It should read like a conversation across a
  table, not a lecture about branding in general.
- When a point can be aimed at the reader, aim it at the reader. Prefer "as the
  senior leader, you..." over "the senior leader..."

## Punctuation

- **No em dashes.** They read as an obvious AI tell. Use parentheses for asides,
  or split the thought into two sentences.

## What to avoid

- Obvious AI tropes and tells (em dashes chief among them; also hedging filler,
  over-symmetry, and "it's not X, it's Y" pile-ups when overused).
- Don't number ideas, sections, lists, or interface elements unless Brent
  specifically asks for numbering. Let hierarchy come from language and design.

## Process (how we edit together)

- Don't write the piece for Brent. Go through it together, section by section.
- Brent drives. He makes updates and asks for specific sections to be added or cut.
- When Brent makes a tone or style change, sweep the rest of the post for the same
  pattern so the whole piece agrees, and record the rule here for future posts.
