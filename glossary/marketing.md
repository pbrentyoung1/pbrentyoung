Marketing is the generous and disciplined work of understanding the right people, telling honest stories that resonate, earning trust, and helping them move toward meaningful change.

Marketing does not mean manipulation, interruption, pressure, or making an organization look more impressive than it is. At its best, marketing listens with empathy, focuses on the people who can genuinely be served, tells the truth clearly, earns permission through trust, and invites people into next steps that are actually good for them.

## In business

Marketing helps the right people recognize what is possible, understand why it matters, and take a meaningful next step toward who they are seeking to become.

## In the church

Marketing should never be reduced to promotion. It helps people recognize: this is for me, these people understand something I am carrying, there is a next step I can take, and this story invites me toward who God is calling me to become.

## What remains true

Marketing serves people before it serves metrics. It tells honest stories that resonate and invites meaningful change.
