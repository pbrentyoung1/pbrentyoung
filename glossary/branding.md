Branding is the visible and verbal expression of identity, mission, and story.

Branding includes logos, colors, typography, photography, language, visuals, environments, and design systems. But it is far wider than the work of a design team. Branding is every intentional signal a church sends about who it is: how the congregation dresses on Sunday, casual or formal; whether the pastor preaches in a robe or in jeans and a tee; from a pulpit or with an iPad; whether the music is liturgical hymns, contemporary worship songs, or something in between; the wording on the signage; the feel of the lobby; the way a greeter says hello at the door. All of these are branding choices.

This is why "we don't do branding" is never quite true. A church that has never opened a brand guide is still branding constantly, only by default instead of on purpose. Branding matters because expression shapes perception. But branding is not Brand. A church can update every visual element and still carry the same unclear Brand if the underlying story, experience, and trust do not change.

## In business

Branding gives strategy visible and verbal form so people can recognize, remember, and trust what an organization stands for.

## In the church

Branding helps a church express its identity, mission, and story clearly. It should serve the truthful story of who God has called the church to be, not create a costume for something it is not.

## What remains true

Brand is perception. Branding is expression. Branding creates signals, but Brand is formed through experience.
